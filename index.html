Values-Aligned AI

How to think about and build with AI from first principles — so that the tools serve your life, not the other way around.

Most AI advice is about doing more, faster. This course is about something different.

AI tools change constantly. What worked six months ago is already outdated. If you build your practice around any specific tool or system, you'll be rebuilding it every year. What doesn't change is how you think — and how clearly you can articulate what you actually want.

This is a course about that: developing a first-principles relationship with AI that transcends any particular platform, so that whatever comes next, you're ready for it.

We've each built AI systems for our own lives — not as tech experiments, but as genuine practices of aligning our tools with our deepest intentions. We've learned that the most powerful thing you can do is not learn a better prompt. It's learn to be clearer about what you actually desire.

Two Principles

Principle One

Go up a layer of abstraction. Don't learn the tool — learn to think at the level above it. That's what makes you adaptable as everything changes.

Principle Two

Get the AI to ask you questions. The AI's job is not to give you answers — it's to evoke your clarity. Learning to use it this way transforms the whole relationship.

Underneath both of these is one core skill: speaking the truth of what you want. The more clearly you can articulate your actual desires — not what you think you should want, but what you genuinely long for — the more powerfully AI can serve you. Clarity is the leverage point.
